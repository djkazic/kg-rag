The Lightning Network (LN) is designed to enable faster and cheaper transactions than the Bitcoin blockchain.
The Lightning Network was launched in 2018.
It consists of a network of nodes connected via payment channels to facilitate transactions.
Acts as a second-layer protocol on top of Bitcoin.
Allows two parties to transact multiple times without committing all transactions to the Bitcoin blockchain.
Transactions can be settled instantly.
Significantly lower transaction fees compared to on-chain Bitcoin transactions.
Capacity is measured in BTC locked in its channels.
Uses smart contracts (HTLCs) to enforce the rules and secure transactions.
Maintains Bitcoin's ethos of decentralization.
